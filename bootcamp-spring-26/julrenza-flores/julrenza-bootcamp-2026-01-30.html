Where I Started

Two interviews, two different problems

I interviewed Mika, a freshman commuter who hates paying for study apps, and Jenny, a senior pre-med student drowning in requirements.

Mika's problem felt small — she just uses free tools instead. But Jenny's problem had real stakes.

"It's kind of hard to just go to places and be like, hey, do you offer shadowing? Because most of the time they're like, no, we don't do it."

Jenny needs 200+ hours just to apply to med school. And nobody tells her how to get them.

2/5

What I Discovered

The shadowing system is broken

200+
hours required for med school
50/50
success rate on cold outreach

Jenny's current approach:

  • Browse billboards in the science building
  • Cold message on LinkedIn/Handshake
  • Wait 1-2 weeks for responses
  • Get rejected half the time

The real insight: there's a "leveling up" system nobody explains. You need starter hours to get intermediate opportunities to get clinical access.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

MedPath: Know What You Qualify For

What if pre-med students could plug in their hours and instantly see what's next?

  • Input: hours, experience type, skills gained
  • Output: "You qualify for X" + "Y more hours unlocks Z"
  • Bonus: auto-generate resume bullets

The key insight: Jenny doesn't need more opportunities listed. She needs to know which ones she actually qualifies for based on where she is right now.

No talking to people required — just plug in your experience and see your path.

4/5

Where I'm At

Ready to build and test

Next steps:

  • Build input form (20 min)
  • Create tier logic: starter → intermediate → clinical
  • Design output that shows next qualified opportunity
  • Test with Jenny at 4pm

The validation question:

"If you could plug in all your Red Cross hours and shadowing, and it told you exactly what you qualify for next — would you use that?"

"I thought the problem was finding opportunities.
It's actually knowing which ones you're ready for."
