Where I Started

The Interview That Changed Everything

I talked to Aaron Lloyd, an advisor at SDSU, about his recent move to San Diego.

What he told me:

"I had to look on dozens of websites and keep track of everything in a huge spreadsheet just to find a neighborhood I'd want to live in."

Moving across states wasn't just stressful—it was a full-time research project.

2/5

What I Discovered

The PRE-Move Problem

The friction wasn't the actual moving. It was everything before:

  • Zillow for housing prices
  • Niche.com for school ratings
  • Google Maps for commute times
  • Reddit for "what's it really like"
  • Crime maps, walkability scores, local amenities...

All tracked in a monster spreadsheet that took weeks to build.

3/5

The Aha Moment

What If It Was All In One Place?

The insight hit us mid-interview:

"Why is Aaron visiting 12 different websites when all this data could live in one dashboard?"

Move Maven brings it all together—neighborhoods, prices, schools, commutes, vibe—so you can actually compare places instead of drowning in tabs.
