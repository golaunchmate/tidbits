Where I Started

Looking for problems in academia

I went into discovery interviews thinking I'd find something around student life or campus logistics.

Instead, I sat down with Amanda — an anthropology professor who's been at SDSU since 2023 after 20+ years in Australia.

She's a first-gen college grad who knew at age 7 she wanted to be an anthropologist. She works with indigenous communities in northern Australia on land and sea rights.

I asked her what's hard about her work life here...

2/5

What I Discovered

International faculty are lost on US taxes

$400-600
annual CPA cost (2x Australia)

Amanda's on an H1B visa. The tax situation is a mess:

  • Few CPAs understand international worker rules
  • Contradictory info online for foreign workers
  • Complex HR decisions she doesn't understand
  • Tax jargon feels like a foreign language

Her previous CPA firm dropped individual clients. She found her current one through word-of-mouth from other international faculty.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

A trust gap, not just a tax gap

Amanda doesn't want TurboTax or H&R Block. She wants someone who understands her situation and communicates with patience.

"Faculty from China, Korea, Australia, UK — generally intelligent people with no tax comprehension."

The friction isn't in doing taxes. It's in finding someone trustworthy who actually gets the H1B complexity.

Amanda's direct words:

"Great job market for somebody to take up."
4/5

Where I'm At

Mapping the solution space

The biggest pain is PRE-consumption — finding the right person, not the tax work itself.

My angle: use accounting as a technical foundation to help mission-driven professionals focus on their actual work.
