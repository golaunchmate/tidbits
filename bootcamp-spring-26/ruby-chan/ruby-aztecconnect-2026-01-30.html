Where I Started

The Interview

Talked to a transfer student in her second semester at SDSU. She works with prospective students daily and transferred from a community college.

Her Reality

"It's a huge campus, it's hard to find community."

She found her clubs through one event (Admitted Student Day) and searching Instagram. But she admitted: niche programs are nearly invisible.

2/5

What I Discovered

The Real Problem

"Very niche programs... not all people know of. So it's hard to find those very small communities."

SDSU has 400+ clubs. Most students only know the big ones. The discovery moment happens once (orientation or Admitted Student Day) — miss it and you're on your own.

The Friction

Transfer students skip freshman orientation entirely. They get dropped into a 35,000-student campus with no map to community.

3/5

What We Built

AztecConnect

A quiz-based club matchmaker with all 400+ SDSU clubs in the database.

  • Take a quick quiz about your interests and style
  • Get matched to clubs by percentage fit
  • Browse the full list filtered by your preferences

The Solution

Instead of wandering or searching blindly, students get personalized matches in 2 minutes.
