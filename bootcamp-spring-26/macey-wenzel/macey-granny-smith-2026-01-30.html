Where I Started

The Hypothesis

I had this idea: Granny Smith Market — a pop-up farmers market inside senior living communities.

Fresh produce. On-site. No trips to the grocery store.

But I needed to validate: Is grocery access actually a problem? Would anyone care?

So I picked up the phone and called Lanterncrest Senior Living.

2/5

What I Discovered

The Real Problem

Carolina, a 5-year employee at Lanterncrest, told me something I didn't expect:

"It's more like them having to load the grocery — they're not up for that most of the time."

It's not about access. It's about physical effort.

  • They have transportation services
  • They order from Amazon, Walmart, Costco
  • But loading groceries is the barrier

Plus: "They're here 24/7... some don't have cars, some don't have a driver's license anymore."

They crave experiences that bring the outside world IN.

3/5

The Validation

Green Lights

Carolina's reaction to my idea:

"I don't think your idea is that far off — they'll probably enjoy it because they don't have to go to the grocery store."

✓ Handcraft markets already get "big turnout" — pop-up format works

✓ She loved the intergenerational angle

✓ She offered to connect me to activities coordinators

Contacts secured:

  • Carolina (front desk champion)
  • Erica (Activities - Pointe building)
  • Plaza building activities team
4/5

Where I'm At

Next Steps
