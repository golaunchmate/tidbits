The Interview

Nick's Elevator Problem

Met Nick — kinesiology senior living on the 5th floor. Both elevators in his building have been broken since December.

He went door-to-door organizing neighbors. Found a lawyer. Did everything right.

"All that work accumulated to nothing. They just see us as money."

The lawyer told him: lease doesn't guarantee elevator access. No legal recourse. Dead end.

2/5

The Insight

The Real Problem Isn't Elevators

Nick's frustration wasn't really about stairs. It was about powerlessness.

  • Weeks spent finding a lawyer
  • Only to learn he had no legal standing
  • Community organized but no way to apply pressure
"How do we take that community aspect and put that force towards making a message?"

Tenants have no playbook for what to do when the law can't help.

3/5

The Solution

TenantPath

Legal triage that doesn't leave you empty-handed.

  • Describe your issue, upload your lease
  • AI checks state + local housing codes in minutes, not weeks
  • Legal standing? → Connect to tenant lawyers
  • No legal standing? → Plan B playbook: organize, escalate, apply pressure
