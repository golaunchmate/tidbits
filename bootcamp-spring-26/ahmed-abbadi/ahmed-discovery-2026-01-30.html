Where I Started

I went wide on purpose

Instead of assuming I knew the problem, I ran 4 discovery calls across completely different students:

Engineering fraternity member

Nursing student fighting for residency spots

Civil engineering sophomore at UCSD

Journalism/advertising student dealing with friend drama

I wanted to find patterns, not confirm assumptions.

2/5

What I Discovered

5 pain themes emerged

Professors don't teach — research-focused, 50% pass rates, students rely on peers

Career competition feels rigged — networking beats hard work

Going solo feels awkward — attending events alone is discouraging

Making friends is harder than it should be — loneliness, trust issues

Peer networks = unfair advantage — fraternity vs no fraternity

"Many people are lonely and don't know where to start."
3/5

The Real Opportunity

Social connection has the most heat

The journalism student didn't just describe the problem — she lit up when I mentioned a potential solution.

✓ Explicit enthusiasm for "friend-finding" concept

✓ Emotional weight behind the pain

✓ Said she would actually use it

The other themes are real, but they're structural — hard to solve at the student level. Social connection is solvable.

4/5

Where I'm At

Ready for solution ideation

I've got a problem with signal. Now I need to:

Break down the friend-making journey (pre/post/consumption)

Generate 2-3 distinct solution approaches

Pick one to prototype

Go back to my interviewee and test willingness to pay

"I thought I'd find an academic problem. I actually found a loneliness problem."
