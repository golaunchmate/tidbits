Where I Started

Two interviews, looking for pain

I talked to members of a Christian fraternity about their challenges. First conversation surfaced some stuff about maintaining "dry chapter" culture, but nothing sharp.

Then I interviewed a former frat president who works at the library. That's when things got interesting.

2/5
What I Discovered

The personalization trap

This guy was DMing 50-100 potential recruits before every rush event. Individually. By hand.

30-60
minutes per event
50-100
DMs each time

He tried Flare (mass messaging app) but stopped using it. His words:

"It didn't feel as personal. People don't feel like they're talking to a robot... They don't feel like I'm being seen."
3/5
The Real Opportunity

Authenticity at scale

The existing solution (Flare) solves speed but kills authenticity.

He rejected it for anything that mattered. The gap:

There's no solution that gives both — personalized-feeling outreach at scale.

This isn't just a frat problem. It's the same tension for:

  • Recruiters messaging candidates
  • Salespeople doing outreach
  • Community managers
  • Creators responding to fans
4/5
Where I'm At

Next: Solution ideation

Problem validated. Now mapping the full journey — what happens before, during, and after sending all those DMs.

Looking for the specific friction points where a solution could live.

"I thought I was looking for any campus problem. I actually found a universal tension between being human and being efficient."
