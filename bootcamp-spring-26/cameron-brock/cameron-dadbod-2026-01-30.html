Where I Started

I thought busy dads wanted convenience above all else. My idea: single-serve protein packets combining whey, creatine, and collagen. Just add water - no more scooping from multiple containers.

The assumption felt obvious: everyone hates the hassle of measuring supplements. Save time, reduce mess, get perfect nutrition.

But I spent weeks building without talking to actual users.

2

What I Discovered

Maya at the gym completely flipped my understanding. When I pitched convenience, she said: "Convenience would actually make it worse for me."

The measuring isn't a bug - it's a feature. "It's therapeutic... part of my morning identity... like a small daily craft."

I wasn't competing against inconvenience. I was competing against a valued morning ritual.

3

The Real Opportunity

Maya identified my actual market for me: "The parents. The people who come in stressed and late. Or people new to supplements who feel overwhelmed."

Two distinct segments:

  • Time-starved parents: Want the benefits but have no ritual time
  • Overwhelmed beginners: Need simplicity to get started

Plus a potential wedge: travel packets for the ritual crowd when they're away from home.

4

Where I'm At

Targeting stressed parents at the gym next. Looking for people rushing between work and pickup, or newcomers asking staff about supplement basics.

Testing hypothesis: Does the time-pressed segment actually want pre-mixed convenience, or do they have their own solutions?

Maya taught me that customer discovery isn't about validating your assumptions - it's about letting users teach you what you don't know.

"I thought I needed to solve inconvenience. I actually needed to find people who experience inconvenience as pain, not pleasure."
