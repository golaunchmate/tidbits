Discovery Phase

The Problem We Found

Interviewed a math major who tutors. The insight:

"Math trauma starts early and compounds forever."

  • Before class: Kids already believe "math is hard"
  • During: Abstract, disconnected from anything they care about
  • After: One bad grade = "I'm not a math person"

Our Reframe

Go upstream. Don't fix college struggles — prevent them in childhood.

2/5

The Journey

From Bold to Buildable

First idea: AI deepfakes of celebrities (Ariana Grande, Jake Paul) teaching math in relatable contexts.

✗ Legal risk with deepfakes
✗ Hard to prove learning outcomes

The pivot: Keep the core insight — math needs to feel personally relevant — but find a buildable path.

✓ Same insight, lower risk

3/5

Final Solution

Personalized Math Game

A video game that teaches math — themed to what each player actually cares about.

  • On first login: asks for your interests
  • Game themes adapt to YOU (sports, music, fashion, gaming)
  • Starts with kids, scales to advanced levels for older players
  • Built-in retention loops (it's a game, not homework)

Why It Works

Relevance + engagement + progression = kids actually want to play.
