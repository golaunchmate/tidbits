What We Found

Problems everywhere

  • Journalism student: "I want a dating app for friends"
  • Engineering student: "Professors are impatient, I rely on TAs"
  • Nursing student: "Can't stand out, system feels rigged"
  • Fraternity member: "Peer support is the only way to survive"
  • International professor: "I pay $600 for taxes nobody understands"

Pattern emerged: students without peer networks struggle academically.

3/5

The Tension

Too many problems, not enough time

Three interviews pointed to the same insight:

"Professors don't care → students with peer networks survive → students without them struggle"

The fraternity already solved this — but it's gatekept behind Greek life.

Meanwhile, the professor interview revealed a completely different business: international faculty tax services — niche, underserved, high willingness to pay.

Decision fork: validated pattern vs. real business opportunity.

4/5

Where We're At

Choosing our lane

Top contenders:

  • → Peer Academic Support — most validated, buildable today
  • → International Faculty Taxes — real pain, clear revenue

Next step: Pick one and build.
