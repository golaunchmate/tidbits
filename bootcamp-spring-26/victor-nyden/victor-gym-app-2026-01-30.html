Where I Started

Three interviews, looking for pain

We talked to students about their daily campus life. One had epilepsy and couldn't drive. Another was satisfied with everything. A third was a grad student/TA who mentioned the gym being too crowded.

At first, "crowded gym" felt weak. People can just go at different times, right?

But we kept digging.

2/5

What I Discovered

The problem isn't crowding—it's blind trips

The real friction happens BEFORE you get there. You walk 15 minutes to the gym, find it packed, and either suffer through it or leave.

15-30
minutes wasted per bad trip
0
ways to check capacity first

Students are making decisions with no information.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

Real-time capacity + smart timing

We combined two approaches:

  • See NOW — Live busyness levels (% full, trend arrows)
  • See BEST TIME — "Usually quietest at 2pm" based on patterns

One glance answers: "Should I go now?" and "When should I go today?"

Starting with the ARC gym. Nail one location, then expand.

4/5

Where I'm At

Next: Validate the data source

  • Ask ARC front desk if they track capacity
  • Explore gym swipe data access
  • Build quick prototype if data exists
  • Test with original interviewees

Biggest risk: Getting accurate, real-time data without building hardware.
