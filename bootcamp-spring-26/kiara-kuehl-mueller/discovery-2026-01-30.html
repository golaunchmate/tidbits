Where I Started

Looking for real campus problems

Went out to interview students about their daily friction. Talked to Jake Rodriguez (freshman from the Bay Area) and Sophia & Charles (German exchange students).

Expected to hear about classes, food, or dorm life.

Instead, transportation kept coming up.

What I Discovered

Cars don't fit student life

$20
Uber for 15 min
$500/mo
rental cost
4 hrs
Zipcar minimum for hiking

Students want spontaneous, short trips—martial arts (45 min), quick hikes (20-min drive).

Current options force you to pay for time you don't need.

German students mentioned "Miles"—cars distributed like scooters, pay-per-mile. That model doesn't exist here.

Where I'm At

Breaking down the journey

Now mapping the pre/post/consumption framework to find where the real friction lives:

  • What happens BEFORE Jake can leave campus?
  • What happens AFTER he's done with his activity?
  • Where does he sound most frustrated?

Next: Generate 2-3 solution approaches that fit how students actually want to use cars.

"The gap isn't 'no cars'—it's that current options don't match how students actually move."
