The Discovery

Students are stranded on campus

Interviewed Jake Rodriguez (freshman, Bay Area) and Sophia & Charles (German exchange students).

Jake wants to go to martial arts. 60-minute class, 15-minute drive away.

Every trip = solving the same puzzle twice: How do I get there cheap and fast? How do I get back?

2/5

Nothing fits short, spontaneous trips

✗ Zipcar: hourly booking, doesn't fit 45-min class

✗ Uber: fast but expensive

✗ Trolley: cheap but slow, requires leaving early

✗ Buy a car: unrealistic for 1-2 semester stay

3/5

The Insight

This is already solved—in Germany

Sophia & Charles mentioned "Miles"—a car-sharing service where:

  • Cars distributed throughout city like scooters
  • Pay per mile, not per hour
  • Unlock via app, drop off anywhere in zone

The model fits exactly how people want to use cars: short, spontaneous, flexible.

4/5

The Decision

Building: SD Miles

Pay-per-mile cars distributed across San Diego.

  • No hourly minimums
  • Unlock via app
  • Drop off anywhere in the city zone
  • Price that works for students and young professionals

Biggest risk: Can the unit economics work at affordable price points?

"The model isn't broken. It just doesn't exist here yet."
