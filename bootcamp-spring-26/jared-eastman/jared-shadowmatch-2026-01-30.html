Where I Started

We went looking for student problems on campus

Found a senior premed student willing to talk. She's juggling 20 units per semester, volunteers at Red Cross, and is trying to get into med school.

We asked about her biggest challenges. Expected to hear about studying or time management.

That's not what she complained about.

What I Discovered

Finding shadowing opportunities is broken

"It's kind of hard to just go to places and be like, hey, do you offer shadowing? Because most of the time they're like, no, we don't do it."

Her current system:

  • Browsing physical billboards in the science building
  • Cold outreach on LinkedIn/Handshake
  • Waiting 1-2 weeks for responses

The Real Opportunity

A verified database of shadowing-friendly doctors

The friction isn't the shadowing itself — it's finding places that actually say yes.

What if premed students could see:

  • Which clinics/doctors accept shadows
  • Requirements and how to apply
  • "Starter-friendly" tags for beginners
  • Crowdsourced from students who got in

✓ Solves the discovery problem

✓ Warm data instead of cold outreach
