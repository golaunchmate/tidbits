Where I Started

Hunting for real problems

Started the day interviewing strangers — a student at Starbucks, IT staff at the library, people in financial aid and registration.

First interviews surfaced everything from Southwest's chaotic new boarding policy to landlords throwing out belongings to Duo authentication headaches.

Lots of pain points. But which one had legs?

2/5

What I Discovered

The pattern across every department

Talking to IT, financial aid, and registration revealed the same friction:

  • Students don't know which portal to check
  • Critical info gets buried in email
  • Financial aid lives in a separate system from Canvas
  • IT issues require physical visits to the help desk

The information exists. It's just scattered everywhere.

The Real Opportunity

Mint for student life

Just like Mint unified bank accounts, cards, and bills into one financial dashboard — students need one place for:

  • Canvas notifications
  • Financial aid deadlines
  • Registration alerts
  • IT self-service fixes

The pitch

"We're not replacing SDSU's systems — we're building the layer on top that organizes what matters."

4/5

Where I'm At

Building The Student Hub

Solution chosen: Centralized dashboard that aggregates notifications and action items from every SDSU system.

Next step: Prototype a clickable mockup and validate with students.

Key question to test: "Would you check this daily? What would make you NOT use it?"

"I thought students needed better IT help. I realized they needed one place to find everything."
