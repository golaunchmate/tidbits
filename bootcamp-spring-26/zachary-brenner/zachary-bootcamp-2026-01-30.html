Finding chaos in conference coordination

January 30, 2026

1/5

Where I Started

Looking for a real problem

Went into discovery interviews with no preconceived ideas. Found a marketing coordinator at SDSU's College of Business who runs three major conferences including the Dean's Conference for all business deans in California.

Asked her one question: "How do you stay organized?"

Her answer: "I don't."

2/5

What I Discovered

Multi-university coordination is chaos

The Dean's Conference spans UCSD, USD, and CSU systems. Every institution uses different tools—Google Drive, Dropbox, various platforms. Files get lost. Formats break.

"I ask for dietary restrictions every single time. I want to store that somewhere so I can just refer back to it."

She wants a "file of humans" she can send when stakeholders ask who attended.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

Her pain = validated market gap

Did competitive research. Found the event management market is $8-17 billion, but:

Enterprise tools cost $79K/year (Cvent)
Academic-specific tools charge $799-4,999/event
No one does multi-institutional coordination
Middle market (50-500 attendees) underserved

Her exact problem—coordinating across universities with persistent attendee data—is a documented gap that existing solutions don't fill.

4/5

Racing to validate

Building prototype in Lovable right now
Persistent attendee profiles + university dashboards
Demo to her by 1:30 PM today
