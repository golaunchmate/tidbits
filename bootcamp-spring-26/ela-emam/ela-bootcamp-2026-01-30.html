Where I Started

I interviewed an SDSU employee who relocated from Indiana

He moved to San Diego 1.5 years ago. His wife came out 2 months earlier to scout neighborhoods.

Her method? Airbnb-hop through different areas—a week in Ocean Beach, a week in North Park—just to get a feel for each place.

"I want word of mouth from actual residents, not landlord descriptions."

They eventually settled on Normal Heights. But the process was expensive, exhausting, and full of guesswork.

2/5

What I Discovered

The real pain is before you find a place

The friction points were clear:

  • Listings scattered across Zillow, Apartments.com, Homes.com
  • No trusted "word of mouth" from real residents
  • Manual Google Doc just to compare options
  • Had to physically live in neighborhoods to understand them
3/5

The Real Opportunity

Yelp for neighborhoods, not apartments

Current platforms help you find units. Nobody helps you find your place—the neighborhood that actually fits your life.

The concept:

  • Real resident reviews on neighborhoods
  • Vibe questionnaire (walkability, avg age, quiet vs. lively)
  • Community threads for each area
  • Search by what people actually say

Type "dog-friendly + walkable + young crowd" → get Normal Heights, with reviews from people who live there.

4/5
