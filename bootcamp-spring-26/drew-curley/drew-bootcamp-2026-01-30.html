Where I Started

Hunting for problems in senior living

My partner and I had a hunch: something's broken with food in retirement communities.

We'd heard complaints across different facility types—luxury places, independent living, assisted care. The pattern kept showing up.

Today I got on the phone with Matthew, a manager at Waterford, an independent living community with ~100 residents.

Goal: Find out what's really going on.

2/5

What I Discovered

Dining is the #1 complaint factory

50/50
satisfaction split
100+
residents to please

The complaints sound contradictory:

  • "Too salty" vs "not seasoned enough"
  • "Too much chicken lately"
  • "I don't like liver. I don't like veal."

They have a chef with 27 years of experience. The food isn't bad. So what's the real problem?

3/5

The Real Opportunity

It's not about taste. It's about control.

These residents spent decades cooking for themselves, their families. They made it their way.

Now someone else decides what's for dinner. Every day. For the rest of their lives.

The frustration isn't the food—it's the loss of autonomy.

This isn't unique to Waterford. It's structural across the entire senior living industry.

Opportunity: Make food feel personal again—without rebuilding the kitchen.

4/5

Where I'm At

Next: Solution ideation

  • Map the food experience (pre/consumption/post)
  • Find where autonomy can be injected
  • Generate 3 different solution approaches
  • Test which one Matthew's residents would actually want
