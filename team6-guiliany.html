Where We Started

We interviewed a signature collector

Petition and voter registration work — standing on campuses trying to get people to sign. Gets paid per signature.

We asked: How do you know where to be and when?

"No schedule. It's kind of by word of mouth of knowing those veterans that have already done it for 10 years."

He went to community colleges that were completely dead. Hollywood Boulevard? 99 no's, 1 yes in two hours. All because he didn't know the patterns.

2/5

What We Discovered

Campus traffic is invisible

The Real Problem:

Nobody knows when or where students will be — not collectors, not clubs, not event organizers. It's all tribal knowledge locked in veterans' heads.

✗ No public schedule of busy times

✗ No data on foot traffic patterns

✗ Events happen but students don't know

✗ Students miss clubs that match their interests

3/5

The Reframe

We flipped the problem

Original angle: Help collectors find students

Our angle: Help students find what's happening

Key Insight:

If students knew about events tailored to them — at times they're actually free — both sides win. Students discover, orgs get visibility.

4/5

What We're Building

Student-first campus discovery

  • Student profile tied to school email
  • Pulls syllabus to know availability
  • Tailored to major + interests
  • Shows all campus events, clubs, tabling
  • Only notifies when you're actually free
"We thought we needed to help collectors find students.
We realized students need to find what matters to them."
5/5
