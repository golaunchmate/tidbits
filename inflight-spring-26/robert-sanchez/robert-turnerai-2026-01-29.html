Where I Started

I thought conversational flow and UI polish were the game.

Built a chatbot advisor for SDSU students. Focused on making it feel natural, adding follow-up questions, building multi-semester roadmaps with "what-if" scenarios. Polished the interface. Optimized the conversation patterns.

I figured if students could have a natural conversation with an AI advisor who understood their degree requirements, that would be enough to win.

The assumption felt solid. But it wasn't what actually mattered.

2 / 5
What I Discovered

Accuracy is the only thing that matters.

When Tam (our partner at SDSU) tested the tool with real students in December, the signal was immediate: if the AI gets a student's degree requirements wrong, nothing else—not the tone, not the UX, not the cleverness of the conversation—matters.

Students were skeptical until the tool got something right. Then they trusted it. Then they noticed when it was wrong.

1 error
Lost the student

The constraint was parsing—extracting accurate information from inconsistent degree audit formats across different schools. Not conversation flow. Not UI design.

This is the moat.

3 / 5
The Real Opportunity

Build parsing accuracy. Everything else follows.

Once I knew this, everything shifted. Stopped optimizing conversation patterns. Started building a specialized backend team focused exclusively on parsing accuracy across degree audit formats.

90% parsing accuracy = Tam's official pilot endorsement for Fowler College

Fowler pilot proof = CSU system expansion (4 million students)

Parsing expertise = Defensible competitive advantage ($1M+ revenue model)

Incorporated as C-Corp to position for institutional partnerships and venture capital. Made Top 50 in Baylor New Venture Competition (Round 2 results expected early March).

Recruiting a specialized parsing-focused intern to accelerate. Merlyn committed as volunteer Interim CTO to guide the architecture.

Where I'm At

Two months to hit 90% accuracy.

Building toward a clear deadline: 90% parsing accuracy within 60 days, validated on real focus group conversations. That unlocks Tam's endorsement and the official SDSU/Fowler pilot.

Running parallel: finishing my final semester (7 classes), managing part-time work, leading the team. Not overwhelmed—actually energized. Multiple small wins (Baylor Top 50, incorporation, Merlyn's commitment) keeping momentum real.

The path is clear now. Not building a feature-rich app. Building an accuracy engine with institutional credibility that becomes irreplaceable in degree advising.

I thought I needed beautiful conversation. I actually needed perfect parsing.
