The Validation Gap

Shipping fast vs. knowing what works.

A story about building features no one asked you to validate — and the uncomfortable question that changes everything.

Context

The shipping streak

Look at the last three weeks:

March 25
Agent Pokes — autonomous execution with effort visibility
April 1
Non-blocking message queue — Supabase over Redis, ship today
April 13
Discord notifications — meet founders where they have attention
April 14
Discord Remote — full two-way chat, LaunchMate's second client

Four features in three weeks. That's real velocity. Each one well-reasoned, each one documented in a tidy tidbit.

But here's the thing I didn't want to look at:

Zero of those decisions were validated by founder behavior.
2/5
The realization

Building in a vacuum

My weekly product strategy check-in hit different this time. The audit came back empty:

  • No Discord vs. web app usage split
  • No early tester feedback quotes
  • No founder response time data
  • No evidence Discord solved the discovery problem

The tidbits I'd been writing? They documented my reasoning, not my results. Beautiful decision journals. Useless for knowing if the decisions were right.

Here's the uncomfortable pattern: I was treating each feature like a solved problem the moment I shipped it. But shipping isn't solving. Solving is when a founder's behavior changes.

And I had zero data on whether any founder's behavior had changed.

3/5
The trade-off

Velocity vs. truth

There's a real tension here and I want to name it honestly:

Ship fast, iterate → Get something out, learn from usage
Validate before building → Slower, but you build the right thing

I'd been optimizing for the first column. And the argument for it is real — I'm leaving in May, the clock is ticking, and deployed software beats perfect software.

But the check-in forced a hard question: What if I'm just really good at building the wrong things fast?

The whole point of Discord Remote was to solve F1 — founder trust through lower friction. But if no founder has actually used it to chat with their agent... I didn't solve F1. I just built infrastructure that could solve F1.

The gap isn't technical. It's observational.

4/5
What changes now

Kill thresholds

No more shipping without a measurement plan. Every feature from here gets a kill threshold — a specific metric that, if it doesn't hit by a specific date, means we stop investing.

Discord Remote kill threshold

Metric: Founder messages sent via Discord (not notifications received — actual two-way usage)

Threshold: < 10 founder messages by April 22

Action: Kill or pivot hard — the discovery thesis is wrong

Three founder interviews this week: one engaged, one hesitant, one ghost. The question I need answered isn't "do you like Discord?" — it's "when you got that notification, what did you actually do?"

And the question I'm sitting with: What metric would make me kill the thing I just built? If I can't answer that, I'm not building a product. I'm building a portfolio.
