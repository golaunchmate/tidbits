Week 17 — April 22, 2026

Who Is LaunchMate For?

The pivot felt right in the room. But the room had the wrong customer.

A story about building for the person who pays attention vs. the person who pays the bills.

1/5
scroll ↓
Two weeks ago

The connector pivot

April 7 team meeting. Craig, Laura, me. The question: "What is LaunchMate?" after three AI revolutions made our chat interface less special.

The answer we landed on: memory hub, not chat app. Connectors auto-populate from existing tools. Dashboard for advisors. Chat is just one door. "Memory is the moat."

My quote: "I could see there being no chat interface for the founder. Connect, connect, connect. Start working."

It felt like clarity. Like we'd finally named the real problem.

But two weeks later, I'm sitting with something uncomfortable about that meeting.

2/5
The uncomfortable question

Who was in that room?

Three people: Craig (advisor), Laura (intern), me (developer). Zero founders.

And the pivot we designed? Let's be honest about who it serves:

Dashboard

Craig sees what founders are working on without waiting for weekly syncs. Advisors get value.

Connectors

Auto-populated context means founders don't have to type updates. But... do founders want their tools talking to each other through us?

The connector pivot makes Craig's life easier. It makes the incubator run smoother. It gives advisors visibility.

But does it make a founder's life easier? That's the question nobody asked.
3/5
The deeper tension

Two customers, one product

LaunchMate has always had two users:

FoundersAdvisors
WantUnblock me NOWKnow what's happening
FrictionTyping updatesNo visibility
ValueContextual helpProgress tracking

Chat interface → optimized for founders.
Dashboard + connectors → optimized for advisors.

The pivot wasn't wrong. But we designed it from the advisor side of the table, because that's who was in the room. We solved Craig's friction (no visibility) and called it a product strategy.

The missing step: asking a single founder if they want their tools connected through LaunchMate.

4/5
What I'm sitting with

Two unvalidated bets

Right now I'm running on faith twice over:

  • Bet 1: Discord Remote solves founder discovery (shipped April 14, zero usage data collected)
  • Bet 2: Connector architecture is the right pivot (proposed April 7, zero founder validation)

The first bet I can validate this week. Three founder conversations. Simple question: "When you got that Discord notification, what did you actually do?"

The second bet needs a different question: "If LaunchMate could automatically pull context from Claude Code or Cursor — would you want that?"

If the answer is "I don't care" or "I prefer keeping tools separate," then the connector pivot isn't a pivot — it's a mirage. And I'd rather know that now than after building the MCP server.

The question isn't whether memory is the moat. It's whose memory matters.
