Where I Started

My mentors gave me two hypotheses to test:

  • Inertia reducer — people want to go on dates but can't get off the couch
  • Transformation — people go to dinner but want to make it feel like a real date

I thought one of these would be the answer. I just needed to figure out which one.

So I started interviewing potential customers.

2/5

What I Discovered

I interviewed Sean. He's not stuck on the couch. He plans dates all the time.

But here's what he said:

1 hour
planning time
Still
not satisfied

"Sometimes I'm ON the date thinking 'we could have done something better.'"

That's not inertia. That's not transformation.

That's FOMO. That's decision anxiety.

He wants to know it's going to be worth it BEFORE he commits the time and money.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

What Sean Actually Needs

Not speed. Not special touches.

Confidence that this decision is the right one for us.

He even tried ChatGPT for date ideas. Didn't work. Why?

"It doesn't understand my area or my interests."

The problem isn't AI. It's personalization.

  • Knows their preferences (not generic suggestions)
  • Uses real data (not hallucinated restaurants)
  • Builds confidence through conversation

My mentor Neal said it weeks ago: "The magic is the interview process."

Now I get it.

4/5
