WHAT I DISCOVERED

The Instagram post IS the product

Watching user behavior, I realized something: people don't really use the filtering features. They pay $5.99 to get posted to the school Instagram, get likes/comments, and then...

They're done. Mission accomplished.

THE REAL OPPORTUNITY

What if churn = success?

I was thinking about this wrong. Users might be getting exactly what they need in 1-2 months:

  • Pay $6 → Get Instagram post
  • Get validation from future classmates
  • Maybe find roommate/friends
  • Cancel because problem solved

This isn't a social network. It's a curated Instagram placement service for anxious incoming students.

The pricing model doesn't match the behavior.

4 / 5

WHERE I'M AT

Validate before I pivot

Before changing pricing or adding features, I'm sending a 2-minute survey to my 239 churned users:

  • "Did you get what you needed?"
  • If yes → What was it? (validates hypothesis)
  • If no → What were you hoping for? (real problem)

Goal: 30-50 responses to see patterns. Then I'll know if I'm solving the problem too well or not well enough.
