1/5

Where I Started

Neal's instruction was clear: "Install it on 10-20 attorneys immediately."

That was the test. Not "perfect the detection" or "polish the dashboard." Just get it in real browsers and iterate.

I thought I'd send zip files to warm leads and watch them install.

But then we hit the wall:

Can't distribute without exposing our entire codebase OR requiring attorneys to enable developer mode and manually load the extension.

Neither option works. Too much friction. Firms won't allow it. Attorneys won't do it.

So... that's it? Blocked?

2/5

What I Discovered

The Pivot

While we figured out distribution, we didn't stop. We built.

In 4 Days

  • NER model tested and ready to plug in
  • Auth system with Supabase + Resend OTP
  • Stripe integration: webhooks, edge functions, product catalog
  • Pricing locked: $7.99/month, $69.99/year
  • Ticketing system for bugs and feedback
  • Complete onboarding user flow
  • Chrome Web Store account: verified, paid, ready
  • Edge Add-on Store account: verified, paid, ready

The "distribution blocker" forced us to build the entire company infrastructure — not just the product, but the business around it.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

Validation While Building

Didn't wait for distribution. Demoed to 4 lawyers instead of installing.

What They Said

  • All 4 loved it — wanted to use it
  • Wanted more detailed reports
  • Wanted Cloak immediately
  • 2 solo practitioners: wrong fit, but confirmed "this slots in nicely at mid-size firms"
  • 2 firm attorneys: passing info to managing partners

The Tension That Surfaced

Attorneys want manager dashboards to check employee usage.

Our architecture: everything local, data never leaves device.

Trade-off: Sync metadata (risk scores, counts) to cloud? Or stay pure local and lose organizational visibility?
