Where I Started

I thought the blocker was distribution.

Chrome Web Store approval. Edge Add-on Store. Waiting for review. Can't get it on lawyers' computers fast enough.

So I demoed to 4 attorneys instead. All positive. But something was off.

The Pattern

I kept starting with: "Do you use AI?"

Solo practitioners said "not for me." Firm attorneys were interested. But there was a hesitation I couldn't explain.

Thursday morning, Craig and Neal told me what was happening.

2/5

What I Discovered

The Pitch Problem

Neal: "By using ['Do you use AI?'] as your starting sentence, you made this sound like a tool to stop people from using AI."

That's why lawyers got defensive. They thought I was offering to monitor them. Police their AI usage.

Solo practitioners don't want to monitor themselves. Of course they said no.

The Reframe

It's not about AI. It's about personal information.

New Opening

"What stops you from putting personal information in the wrong places?"

The Tool Validation

Craig: "This is one of the more compelling demos I've seen. I look at it and I go, of course I want this on my shoulder. 100%."

The tool is good. The pitch is the problem.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

Distribution Shift

Neal told me to stop waiting for Chrome Web Store.

Instead: Collison installation.

What Patrick Collison Did (Stripe)

Sat with first 200 customers personally. Installed Stripe himself.

"I will not leave your side until I get you up and running."

The New Plan

  • Go to lawyers' offices
  • Install the tool myself
  • Don't leave until they're running
  • Do this for the first 200 customers

Website Overhaul

  • New headline: "Privacy layer for AI"
  • Add checkout flow — capture credit cards
  • Kill the confusing "Thinkers belong to big thoughts" tagline
