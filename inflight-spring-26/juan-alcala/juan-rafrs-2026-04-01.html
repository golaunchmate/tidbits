Where I Started

Testing Marina Repair Shops

Went to Shelter Island to talk to the people who fix boats for a living.

Logic was: They know repairs. They'd see the value.

Showed them the kit. They liked the idea.

But they wouldn't buy it.

2/5

What I Discovered

Wrong Customer, Right Problem

The repair shops said:

"This is more expensive for us in the long run. We already have proper methods."

But then they added:

"For a boater? This would be priceless if it gets them out of a pinch."

The insight: The kit isn't for professionals. It's for the person stranded offshore with no other option.

Repair pros have shops. Boat owners have nothing.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

Clarity on Who and Why

This validates everything from Feb 20. The pivot holds.

PROBLEM

We help boat owners get home when something breaks offshore.

SOLUTION

A carbon-fiber repair kit that bonds to metal in 15 minutes — no welding, no power, no skills required.

Target locked: Boat owners, racing crews, charter captains. Not repair shops.

4/5

Where I'm At

Find the Stranded

Next moves:

  • Coronado Beer Can racing series — racers get stuck mid-race
  • Freedom Boat Club members — not the shop, the people on the water
  • Charter captains — their livelihood depends on staying operational

The Shelter Island conversations were a detour. But they pointed me back to the real customer.

"I thought repair shops would want this. They don't. The people they rescue do."

5/5
