WHERE I STARTED

Anchored to Welding

Two weeks ago, I was running four experiments to find desperate customers. Go to marinas. Hit manufacturing hubs. Talk to machine shops. The framing was always the same: "This is an alternative to welding."

Problem was, welders weren't desperate. They had equipment, skills, and options. I was pitching a solution to people who didn't have a problem.

Neal's question stuck with me: "Find the situation where your trade-offs are actually advantages."

2/5

WHAT I DISCOVERED

Boat People Already Get Composites

Went to the boatyard. Started talking about carbon fiber repair.

The repair guy didn't need convincing. He's been using fiberglass for years. Carbon fiber is just 3x stronger — same concept, better material.

He was actively fixing rudders. Named specific use cases. Then he said the thing:

"Boaters do two or three day trips to Catalina. Something breaks out there, they usually have to send a towboat."

3/5

THE REAL OPPORTUNITY

We're Not Selling Repairs. We're Selling Insurance.

Craig's observation: "For the first time, you've been talking about your product without anchoring it to welding."

The welding framing was holding me back. Boats don't care about welding — they care about fiberglass, which they already understand.

Neal's insight: "Every boat has a first aid kit. You just feel better knowing this is on your boat."

That's the pivot:

  • FROM: Sell to repair guys as "better technique"
  • TO: Sell to boat owners as "emergency kit"

The kit might never be used. That's OK. It's insurance.

4/5

WHERE I'M AT

Finish the Product

Boatyard guy asked me to come back. Craig's directive: "Show up with a finished product."

Current kit cost: $35. Target price: $149 (4.3x markup, still 20x cheaper than replacement).
