Where I Started

Testing the Everyday People Market

I thought: Fix It Fair would validate demand from regular people who need repairs.

Setup at the engineering courtyard. Four demos, 30 minutes each. People brought broken items, tried the kit.

The tech worked. The kit worked.

But something felt off.

What I Discovered

The Replace vs Repair Economy

4
demos completed
0
viable customers

People kept saying: "I don't have anything worth fixing versus replacing."

"The US is a replace economy. If it breaks, you buy a new one."

Everyday people don't have the repair mindset. They don't have expensive enough things that justify a $149 kit.

But this wasn't a failure. It was validation.

3/5

The Real Opportunity

Boat People. Validated.

My Feb 20 pivot to maritime was right. Fix It Fair just proved the inverse.

Boat owners are different:

  • They have expensive things worth repairing
  • They're stranded when something breaks
  • They'll pay $149 for peace of mind

NEW: Freedom Boat Club — membership-based fleet rental. Multiple locations, each with repair needs. High revenue pressure when boats are down. Enterprise B2B angle.

"This is an in-person sale. You want to see people in the eyes and have them tell you why they don't like your box." — Cathy
