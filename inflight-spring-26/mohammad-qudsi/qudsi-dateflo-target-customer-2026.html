1/5

Who I Thought It Was

When I started, I thought my customer was the "couch-sitter":

  • Couples who never get around to planning dates
  • People stuck in routine, need a push to act
  • The problem is inertia — starting is too hard

This was Hypothesis B from my mentors: "Help people go from couch to action."

Turns out: This wasn't the customer at all.

2/5

Who It Actually Is

My real customer is the "active planner with doubt":

  • Already plans dates — not lazy, not stuck
  • Spends time researching options
  • Lands on something... but isn't confident it's right
  • Experiences FOMO during the date

The problem isn't starting. It's decision confidence.

The insight: They don't need me to find them a date. They need me to tell them it's the right date.

3/5

4/5

The Pattern

What they have in common:

  • ✓ Already in relationships (not single)
  • ✓ Care about making it special
  • ✓ Have pressure — upcoming event, birthday, anniversary
  • ✓ Willing to pay for confidence

What they don't have:

  • ✗ Time to research thoroughly
  • ✗ Confidence in their own choices
  • ✗ Someone who knows what they actually want

What I provide:

  • A 10-minute conversation that extracts what matters
  • A complete date plan they can trust
  • Confidence that "this is the right choice"
"I'm not selling date ideas. I'm selling decision confidence."
