1/6

The Superpower

"The problem is not planning dates. The problem is getting people to open up about what is meaningful."
Craig Lauer · Feb 20, 2026
What I heard: ChatGPT can plan dates. It can't do what you do in that 10-minute conversation. You extract from people what they don't even know they have.

This killed my "build an app" strategy. The value isn't the output — it's the intake.

The shift: Scale me, not the app.

2/6

The Conversation

"The magic was the conversation. If it was just a sheet of paper with recommendations, she'd never use this again."
Craig Lauer · Feb 13, 2026

Craig watched me do a live demo with Cathy. When problems came up — closed restaurant, no parking, she hates sand — she gave me a pass because we were in conversation.

Why this matters: Conversational format creates error tolerance. Pure tech output doesn't get second chances. Relationships do.

The principle: Don't hand off a PDF. Stay involved through date night.

3/6

The Wraparound

"Men have devalued the word date. You give the weight back."
Neal Bloom · Feb 6, 2026

Neal saw that my real value wasn't restaurant picks — it was the small intentional touches:

→ Playlist for the drive
→ Activity after dinner
→ "Bring a backup jacket" reminder
→ Extra apron for cooking dates

The warning: Don't own restaurant picks. You own every failure outside your control. Own the wraparound — that's where you add value.
4/6

The Ugly Baby

"Your baby's ugly."
Craig Lauer · Feb 20, 2026

I was excited about "Tidbits" — menu pictures, extra details, more content per date.

Craig cut through it instantly. The feature didn't solve the core problem. It was a distraction.

The lesson: When a mentor tells you your baby is ugly, believe them. They see something you can't. Kill your darlings and focus on what matters.

What died: Tidbits feature. App automation. One-off $5 model.

5/6

The Rich People Formula

"Take something only rich people can do and democratize it."
Craig Lauer · Feb 20, 2026

Rich people have personal concierges. They don't plan — they have someone who knows them, asks the right questions, and handles everything.

That's DateFlo. Not an app. A relationship.

"Talk to me for 10 minutes,
I'll have a date for you by the end."

That's the product. Everything else is noise.
