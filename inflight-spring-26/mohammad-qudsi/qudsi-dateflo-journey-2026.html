Where It Started

LazyDate — a Pinterest-style platform for date ideas.

I thought the problem was discovery. People can't find good date ideas, so I'd build a platform to help them search and save.

First validation: Sold "$5 plan my date" service to friends. 20-30 prepaid orders in one week.

But here's the thing — I validated a service, not a platform. People paid me to plan dates FOR them, not to use a tool themselves.

The tension that defined the next 2 months

I was building a self-serve app. People were paying for done-for-you service.

2/6

What I Killed

Feb 6, 2026

Ghost Mode Hypothesis

I thought people wanted to hide that they used an app. "Partner can't know I planned this."

Data point: Customer showed partner the PDF. Magic didn't die.

Killed. The problem wasn't shame — it was something else.

Feb 20, 2026

App Automation Strategy

I thought the value was AI-generated date plans. Scale the app, not me.

Craig's insight: "The problem is not planning dates. The problem is getting people to open up about what is meaningful."

Killed. The app can't do what I do in that 10-minute conversation.

Feb 20, 2026

"Tidbits" Feature

Menu pictures, extra details, more content per date.

Craig: "Your baby's ugly."

Killed. Focus on intake, not content.

3/6

What I Discovered

The Sean Interview (Feb 15)

Active dater. Plans dates regularly. Not the "couch-sitter" I thought was the customer.

1 hour

Still unsure

His problem: Decision confidence. He lands on something and thinks "we could have done something better."

Quote: "I'd be willing to spend MORE money if I know what to expect."

The Cathy Demo (Feb 13)

Craig watched me do a live 10-minute planning session. When problems came up (closed restaurant, no parking), Cathy gave me a pass because we were in conversation.

Craig: "The magic was the conversation. If it was just a sheet of paper, she'd never use this again."

4/6

The Real Product

Feb 20, 2026 — Craig + Neal Session

My superpower: The human intake process. I pull out of people what they don't even know they have. ChatGPT can plan dates. It can't do that.

The Pivot

  • Build app to automate planning
  • Self-serve platform
  • $5 per date (transactional)
  • Date Concierge Service

The Model

  • $50/month for 2 custom dates
  • Free first date (credibility builder)
  • 10-minute intake conversation
  • Stay involved through date night (text check-ins)
  • Ongoing relationship (not transactional)

Craig: "I would sign up for that right now."

5/6

Where I'm At

What's Clear

  • Product: Date Concierge — "Talk to me for 10 minutes, I'll have a date for you by the end"
  • Customer: Active daters with pressure (Valentine's, birthdays, anniversaries)
  • Model: $50/month subscription
  • Superpower: Human intake process (not AI automation)

What's Next

  • Convert Craig to first paying customer
  • Perfect the intake process
  • Build evidence through delivered dates
  • Scale me, not the app
"I thought I was building a platform to help people find dates. I'm actually building a concierge service that scales human conversation."
