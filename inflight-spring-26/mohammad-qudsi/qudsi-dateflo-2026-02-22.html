Where I Started

Last week, Craig and Neal gave me two hypotheses to test:

  • Hypothesis A: Do people need help with decision confidence? (Active daters who want better choices)
  • Hypothesis B: Do people need help with inertia? (Couch-sitters who never act)

My plan: Interview customers, deliver more dates, figure out which problem I'm really solving.

I thought I'd spend the week validating hypotheses. I ended up discovering my entire business model.

The Cathy Demo Review

Craig watched me do a live 10-minute date planning demo. When problems came up (closed restaurant, no parking, she hates sand), Cathy gave me a pass because we were in conversation—not just receiving AI output.

The Real Opportunity

Craig/Neal Strategy Session (Feb 20)

Craig: "The problem is not planning dates. The problem is getting people to open up about what is meaningful."

The insight: My superpower isn't the AI recommendations—it's the human intake process. I pull out of people what they don't even know they have.

The Pivot

  • NOT building an app
  • NOT automating the conversation
  • Date Concierge Service — ongoing relationship, subscription model

Business Model

  • Free first date (credibility builder)
  • $50/month for 2 custom dates
  • I stay involved through date night (text check-ins, adjustments)
  • Ongoing relationship (not transactional)

Craig's reaction: "I would sign up for that right now."

The Rich People Formula: Take something only rich people can do (personal concierge) and democratize it.
